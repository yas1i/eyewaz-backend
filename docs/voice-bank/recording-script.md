# EYEWAZ dialect voice — recording script

Goal: capture ~1–2 minutes of clean speech from **one consenting native speaker**
of a dialect, so it can be cloned into an EYEWAZ reading voice.

## Before you record
- One speaker only, a quiet room, no music/TV/echo.
- Phone or laptop mic is fine held ~20 cm away; avoid wind/handling noise.
- Speak **naturally and at a normal pace** — this becomes the reading voice.
- Record as a single file (WAV or MP3/M4A). 1–2 minutes total is plenty.
- Get consent first (see `consent-form.md`) and note the speaker's name.

## What to say (read in the dialect, naturally)
Read the following slowly and clearly. Mix statements, a question, and numbers so
the voice learns range, pauses and intonation.

1. A short self-introduction: your name, your city, and one sentence about your day.
2. "Reading is hard for many people. With EYEWAZ, listening is easy."
3. Count aloud: one, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten.
4. Say today's date and the time in words.
5. Two everyday sentences, e.g. "Please take your medicine after breakfast." and
   "The shop opens at nine in the morning and closes at six in the evening."
6. A question, spoken as a question: "Have you had your tea yet?"
7. A calm, encouraging closing line: "Take your time. I am here to help you."

Re-record if there's a long stumble. A few natural pauses are good — they help the
clone learn realistic stops.

## After recording
Give the file + the speaker's name + signed consent to the EYEWAZ admin. In the
app: **Account → Voice bank (admin) → pick the dialect → upload the file → tick
consent → Create dialect voice.** It goes live in the dialect picker immediately.
